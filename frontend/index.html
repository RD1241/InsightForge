    Time Series Metrics Quick Guide:
    • MAE (Mean Absolute Error): Represents the average units deviation in predictions. Lower is better.
    • MAPE (Mean Absolute % Error): Standard percentage discrepancy. An error rate of 10% or less represents high forecasting accuracy.
    • R² (R-squared): Explains validation variance fit. 1.0 is a perfect fit, closer to 1.0 is better.

    ML Explainability & Examiner's Viva Guide

    Q1: Why use a chronological train/test split instead of random split?

    Randomly shuffling time series data causes "temporal leakage" (where future dates leak into the past). We split our data chronologically—training on historical periods and validating on the final 30 days—to evaluate the models under realistic forecasting conditions.

    Q2: How does recursive forecasting work for Machine Learning models?

    Autoregressive models (Linear Regression, Random Forest) use lag variables ($t-1, t-7$, etc.). To predict the next 30 days, we do not have actual future sales, so we calculate the prediction for day $t$, append it to our sales timeline buffer, and use it as a feature to predict day $t+1$.

    Q3: How are the 95% forecast confidence intervals calculated?

    Prophet calculates uncertainty bounds natively. For Linear Regression and Random Forest, we compute the residual standard deviation ($\sigma_e$) on the training set: $\sigma_e = \sqrt{\frac{1}{N}\sum(y_t - \hat{y}_t)^2}$. The 95% confidence bands are projected as: $\text{prediction} \pm 1.96 \cdot \sigma_e$.

    Q4: Why compare Prophet with Random Forest and Linear Regression?

    Prophet excels at decomposing additive weekly/yearly seasonality profiles and price discount surges. Random Forest is a non-linear ensemble model that excels at mapping complex, interactive features. Linear Regression serves as our baseline reference model.
